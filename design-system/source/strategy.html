1.2

Audience pillars

Three customer archetypes the brand has to land with on contact. Every page, every ad, written for one of them.

PRIMARY · 62%

The Frustrated Gardener

45–68 · Suburban · Female-leaning

Has been a gardener for 10+ years. Loves hostas, tulips, hydrangeas. Has watched deer eat all three. Has tried Irish Spring soap, fishing line, motion lights. Is ready for something that actually works.

"I just want my garden back."
SECONDARY · 24%

The Property Defender

38–60 · Acreage · Pragmatic

Has 1+ acre. Trees, shrubs, a vegetable patch. Sees deer as a property-management problem more than a personal one. Buys gallons, not bottles. Reads spec sheets.

"Show me the coverage and the cost-per-acre."
TERTIARY · 14%

The Eco-Conscious Buyer

28–45 · Urban-edge · Values-led

Will not use synthetic chemistry near pets, kids, or pollinators. Reads ingredient lists. Pays a premium for natural. Will tell three friends if we deliver.

"Is it really safe? Like, actually safe?"
1.3

Voice — we do, we don't

Plain-spoken, confident, never preachy. The single best test: would a real gardener actually say it out loud?

We sound like →

Confident"It works. Here's how."
Plain"Plant oils. Water. That's it."
Specific"30 days between sprays. 1,200 sq ft per bottle."
Honest"Smells strong for an hour. Then, nothing."
Warm"We've got hostas too."
Useful"Spray a 3-foot perimeter. Reapply after heavy rain."

Not like ←

Salesy"Revolutionary repellent technology!"
Vague"Premium natural ingredients."
Hand-wavy"Long-lasting protection guaranteed."
Hiding"Specially formulated odor profile."
Cold"Optimal coverage solutions for residential properties."
Empty"Performance you can trust."
