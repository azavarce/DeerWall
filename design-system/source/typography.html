FIELD NOTES · ISSUE 04

Why deer leave the hostas alone.

After fifteen years of testing, three plant oils that the trigeminal nerve simply refuses to ignore.

Deer don't read warning labels. They navigate the world by smell, and their olfactory bulb is roughly four times the size of ours. So you can't argue them out of your tulips. You have to make the air around them genuinely unpleasant.

"Three oils. One smells like rotting fruit to a deer. The others reinforce it."

That's the whole game. We blend them at concentrations that read 'predator' to a whitetail and 'damp herb' to a human nose, and we keep the carrier completely inert. Spray it once. The garden smells faintly of rosemary for an hour. The deer remember it for thirty days.
