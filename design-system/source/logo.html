02 · Brand

Logo & lockups.

A wordmark with antlers and a confident wall — the two ideas in the name, made visible. Use the master lockup wherever it fits; the alternates exist for the spots where it doesn't.

2.1

Master logo

The horizontal lockup is the default. Use it on packaging, websites, vehicles, and any signage with horizontal real estate.

DW-LOGO-01 · MASTER

Horizontal primary

"Deer" in orange Beausite Classique with antler glyphs growing from the two e's. "WALL" reversed out of an orange block — a confident, repellent wall. The two halves butt directly without a gap; never separate them.

2.4

Clear space

The minimum breathing room around the logo, defined by the height of the antler — call it X. Nothing — no text, no edge, no other mark — enters the cordon.

X
X
X
X

One antler, all the way around.

Measure the antler height (X). That's the absolute minimum margin on every side of the logo. When in doubt, give it more.

1×Minimum on packaging, business cards, small ads
1.5×Default for most digital and print contexts
2×Hero placement on web and brand campaigns

2.6

Don't

Six common ways to break the logo. Don't.

Don't squishLock the aspect ratio. Hold Shift.
Don't stretchThe proportions are tuned. Trust them.
Don't rotateThe wordmark stays level. Always.
Don't recolorUse approved colorways only.
Don't add effectsNo drop shadows, glows, bevels.
Don't outlineThe logo is solid. Keep it that way.
