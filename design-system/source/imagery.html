6.2

Three subject pillars

Every brand image falls into one of three buckets. Mix them across a campaign — never lean on one alone.

PILLAR 01
The animal

Whitetail deer photographed with respect. Treated in brand orange for hero use, untreated for editorial. Never aggressive, never threatening.

PILLAR 02
The gardener

Real people in the work — hands on plants, low golden-hour light, real expressions. No staged grins, no lifestyle stock clichés.

PILLAR 03
The garden

Hostas, hydrangeas, tomatoes, tulips — what the customer is actually trying to protect. Tight, painterly crops that feel held by hand.

6.4

Treatments

Three treatment recipes. Pick by hierarchy: natural for editorial, orange for hero/campaign, mono for moments where the headline needs to win.

01 · Natural
Untreated. Slight warmth +5, lift shadows. Default for editorial & product.
02 · Brand orange
Multiply Orange-500 at 65%. Hero only. The DeerWall signature.
03 · Mono
Desaturate fully. 15% ink wash. For long-form & press placements.

6.6

Don't

Four image traps. The aesthetic is honest, slightly warm, never digital-feeling.

Don't oversaturatePlants are green. Keep them green.
Don't blur subjectsBokeh in the back, sharp on the hands.
Don't recolor wildOrange treatment only. No purple, blue, etc.
Don't sepiaWe're modern, not nostalgic. Avoid stock-photo filters.
