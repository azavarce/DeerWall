Each slide type has a job, a palette, and a voice. Decks that mix these up feel like decks made by three different people.

11.4

Type & motion

Two pages of rules. Type goes one way, motion goes the other. Both tell the audience to lean forward.

Type · in slide scale

A slide is read from twenty feet away. Type that's "fine" on a laptop is illegible from row 6. Use this scale or up.

Motion · the only two

Slides move two ways: things slide up, or things cross-fade. Anything else looks like a 2007 keynote.

SLIDE-UP · 240MS · EASE-OUT
CROSS-FADE · 400MS · LINEAR

Slide-up is for entrances. Cross-fade is for full-slide transitions. Never mix them in the same deck.

11.5

Pre-flight checklist

Run this before sending the deck. Five minutes here saves the room.

Eight things to check.

Before the export, before the screen-share, before the distributor sees it. Run these eight.

One italic per slideTwo italics fight each other. The italic is the punch line.
Wordmark on every slide footerMono, 9pt, bottom-left, alongside slide number.
Page numbers in mono"03 / 08" — leading zero, slash, total. Always.
No more than 3 colors per slideCounting the background. Two is better.
Real numbers, real namesIf a chart isn't sourced or a quote isn't attributed, kill it.
One headline thesis per slideIf a slide could split in two, it should split in two.
Closer mirrors the openerSame italic, same color block, different sentence.
Export at 1920 × 1080Native deck size. Anything else gets resampled and looks soft.
