# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Exurban / wooded-edge / small-town gardeners (primary) and small-acreage farmers (secondary), reached through paid Google Search ads with intents like "deer ate my hostas," "natural deer repellent," "stop deer eating roses." Targeted geographies are 13 states (FL, AL, GA, MS, TN, VA, NC, OH, NJ, PA, NY, MI, WI, TX) with deer-pressure counties, places like Westchester NY, Loudoun VA, Williamson TN. State of mind on arrival: irritated, recently lost plants, skeptical of "natural" claims because they have tried smelly big-box products that did not work. They want proof, not poetry.

## Product Purpose

DeerWall is an all-natural, pet-safe deer-repellent spray. The landing page is the destination for paid Google Ads traffic and must convert first-time buyers across three SKUs: 32oz Ready-to-Use ($39.97 / 1,350 sq ft), 1 Gallon Ready-to-Use ($99.97 / 5,000 sq ft, most popular), and 1 Gallon Concentrate ($189.97 / 4 acres). Success = adds to cart from the SKU chooser block.

## Brand Personality

Confident, plain-spoken, slightly rural-American. Three words: earned, calm, agricultural-modern. A neighbor who has tried things and finally found one that works, not a marketer. Warm without being precious. Knows the names of plants. Italic emphasis used sparingly on key brand verbs.

## Anti-references

- SaaS-style landing pages (Linear, Vercel-flavored minimal-on-white)
- Big-box outdoor-products visual language (Spectracide, Liquid Fence — chaotic green-red supermarket-shelf design)
- Generic AI-editorial templates (Cormorant drop-cap on a hiking brand, Newsreader-on-cream-with-grain-overlay clichés)
- Lifestyle wellness brands (Goop, Olipop) — too curated, too coastal
- Gradient-on-glass, neon-on-black, any tech-y treatment

## Design Principles

1. **Show, do not claim.** Karen's $75-of-eaten-sunflower-seeds story beats any adjective. Photograph the result; do not promise it.
2. **Earn trust through restraint.** No badges screaming "100% NATURAL!!". One mono label, used once, and let the page back it up.
3. **One decision per fold.** The hero asks one question; the chooser answers it; the proof confirms it; the CTA closes it.
4. **Editorial without the costume.** Use the serif/mono/sans rhythm because the brand earned it, not as decoration. No drop caps, no rule lines, no broadsheet pretension.
5. **Speak the gardener's vocabulary.** Hostas, hydrangeas, roses, tomato beds, sunflowers, not "vegetation" or "outdoor plants."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA across all body copy and interactive controls. Body text uses Ink 800 on Sand 50 (14.8:1, AAA). Orange-500 never carries body text; it passes large-text only. Form fields have visible focus states (orange-500 border). Reduced-motion users: respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — disable any reveal animations, keep transitions at or below 150ms.
