Plain answers

Six questions every gardener asks first.

Is DeerWall safe around dogs and cats?

Yes. DeerWall is formulated with botanical ingredients and no synthetic pesticides. We still recommend letting the spray dry for an hour or two before pets play in treated areas, the same way you'd let any spray settle.

Will rain wash it off?

Light rain and dew, no. The formula bonds to the leaf surface as it dries. After a heavy storm or several days of rain, reapply. New growth that came in after your last spray also needs coverage.

How long does one application last?

About two weeks under typical conditions. Heat, rain, and rapid plant growth shorten it; mild dry weather extends it. Most gardeners settle into a 14-day rhythm.

Will it harm bees, butterflies, or other pollinators?

DeerWall is not an insecticide. The formula targets the senses deer use to evaluate plants, which is a different mechanism from anything that would affect pollinators. Best practice with any spray: apply in early morning or evening when bees and butterflies are not actively foraging, and avoid open blossoms.

Does it smell bad?

DeerWall is essentially odorless. No rotten-egg, no garlic-bomb, no synthetic fragrance masking what's in the bottle. To deer, it reads as "do not eat" for the next two weeks.

Which size should I order first?

If you're treating a single bed or a few featured plants, the 32 oz Ready-to-Use is the right call. If you have multiple beds or a small property, the 1-gallon Ready-to-Use is what most customers pick. For large yards or rural acreage, go straight to the 1-gallon Concentrate.
